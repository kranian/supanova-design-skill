## 1. Typography System

### Design Philosophy

Premium architecture brands use typography to signal quiet authority and sophistication. The 2025-2026 trend favors neo-grotesque sans-serifs for their restrained professionalism, paired with high-contrast serifs for editorial elegance. The goal is to be "either loud or incredibly elegant" — for a craftsman brand like Cheolmoksu, elegant is the correct direction.

### Recommended Font Pairings

#### Option A: Premium Serif + Modern Sans (Recommended for Cheolmoksu)

| Role | Font | Style | Source |
|------|------|-------|--------|
| Headlines / Display | **Cormorant Garamond** | High-contrast serif, inspired by 16th-century Garamond | Google Fonts (free) |
| Body / UI | **Inter** | Neo-grotesque sans-serif, optimized for screens | Google Fonts (free) |
| Accent / Captions | **Inter** (light weight) | Clean, neutral at small sizes | Google Fonts (free) |

Pairing rationale: Cormorant Garamond and Inter have closely matched x-height ratios (0.68 vs 0.73) ensuring visual harmony, and balanced character widths (520 vs 550 units) that work well in mixed layouts. The serif headlines evoke tradition and craftsmanship, while Inter's clarity communicates modern engineering precision.

#### Option B: All Sans-Serif (Minimalist Engineering Focus)

| Role | Font | Style | Source |
|------|------|-------|--------|
| Headlines | **Sohne** | Neo-grotesque, quiet authority | Klim Type Foundry (paid) |
| Body | **Aktiv Grotesk** or **Suisse Int'l** | Professional neo-grotesque | Dalton Maag / Swiss Typefaces (paid) |
| Fallback (free) | **Inter** or **Neue Haas Grotesk** (display) | Available via Google Fonts | Google Fonts |

#### Option C: Korean-Optimized Pairing

| Role | Font | Reasoning |
|------|------|-----------|
| Korean Headlines | **Noto Serif KR** (Bold/Black) | High-contrast serif with proper Korean glyph support |
| Korean Body | **Pretendard** or **Noto Sans KR** | Clean, modern sans-serif optimized for Korean readability |
| English Headlines | **Cormorant Garamond** | Pairs well with Noto Serif KR in weight and contrast |
| English Body | **Inter** | Pairs well with Pretendard/Noto Sans KR in x-height |

### Type Scale

Use a modular scale based on a ratio of 1.250 (Major Third) or 1.333 (Perfect Fourth) for premium feel:

```
Display:     48-72px  (Cormorant Garamond, 300-400 weight)
H1:          36-48px  (Cormorant Garamond, 400-500 weight)
H2:          28-36px  (Cormorant Garamond, 500 weight)
H3:          22-28px  (Inter, 600 weight)
Body Large:  18-20px  (Inter, 400 weight)
Body:        16px     (Inter, 400 weight)
Caption:     13-14px  (Inter, 400-500 weight)
Overline:    11-12px  (Inter, 500-600 weight, letter-spacing: 0.1em, uppercase)
```

### Typography Rules

- Limit to 2-3 typefaces maximum across the entire brand
- Use serif for headings and display, sans-serif for body text and UI elements
- Establish clear hierarchy through weight, size, and spacing — not decoration
- Letter-spacing: Tight (-0.02em) for large headlines, normal (0) for body, wide (+0.05-0.1em) for overlines and captions
- Line-height: 1.1-1.2 for headlines, 1.5-1.7 for body text
- For Korean text: line-height should be 1.6-1.8 for body to accommodate glyph complexity

## 2. Color Palettes

Premium architecture brands lean toward restrained, high-contrast palettes with strategic metallic or warm accents. The palette should communicate: engineering precision (dark neutrals), craftsmanship warmth (natural tones), and trust (deep blues or blacks). For Cheolmoksu's steel house focus, integrating references to raw materials (steel gray, warm wood tones) reinforces the brand story.

## 3. Photography & Visual Storytelling

### Photography Standards

Architecture and construction photography must follow strict standards for lighting, composition, and styling. Every image should tell a story about the spaces and the people who create them.

#### Image Categories for Cheolmoksu

1. **Process Photography** (YouTube-aligned transparency)
   - Raw construction progress shots with dramatic natural lighting
   - Close-ups of steel framing, welding, precision joinery
   - Time-lapse sequences showing structure rising from foundation
   - Workers in action — hands, tools, materials in focus

2. **Completed Project Photography**
   - Golden hour exterior shots (warm light emphasizing materiality)
   - Interior shots with natural light flooding through steel-framed windows
   - Twilight "blue hour" shots with interior warmth glowing outward
   - Detail shots of steel-to-wood transitions, custom fabrication

3. **Craftsman Portrait Photography**
   - Founder/builder in working environment, not posed studio shots
   - Environmental portraits showing real tools, real workspace
   - Hands-at-work imagery reinforcing the "moksu" (craftsman) identity

4. **Material & Texture Close-ups**
   - Raw steel surfaces, welds, brushed metal
   - Wood grain, joint details, precision cuts
   - Concrete, glass, natural stone textures

### Photography Technical Standards

| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| Aspect Ratio | 16:9 (hero), 4:3 (portfolio), 1:1 (grid thumbnails) |
| Minimum Resolution | 2400px wide for hero images |
| Color Treatment | Desaturated with warm undertones; avoid heavy filters |
| Lighting | Natural light preferred; golden hour for exteriors |
| Composition | Rule of thirds; leading lines from architectural elements |
| Post-Processing | Subtle contrast boost, slight warmth (+5-10K), minimal vignette |

### Visual Storytelling Principles

- **Show the process, not just the product**: Aligns with Cheolmoksu's YouTube transparency philosophy
- **People in spaces**: Include occupants or builders to humanize architecture
- **Material honesty**: Highlight raw materials — steel beams, wood, concrete
- **Consistent mood**: Every image should share a color temperature and editing style
- **Before/during/after narratives**: Document transformation from site to structure

## 4. Spacing, Whitespace & Layout Principles

### The Luxury Whitespace Rule

Premium buyers are conditioned to associate space with exclusivity. Generous white space adds an instant feeling of sophistication and signals quality. Research shows users stay on well-spaced pages 15-30% longer, and strategic whitespace around CTAs increases click-through rates by 25%.

### Spacing System

Use an 8px base unit with a geometric progression:

```
4px   (0.25rem)  — micro: icon padding, inline gaps
8px   (0.5rem)   — xs: tight element spacing
16px  (1rem)     — sm: related element groups
24px  (1.5rem)   — md: between form fields, list items
32px  (2rem)     — lg: between content blocks
48px  (3rem)     — xl: between sections on mobile
64px  (4rem)     — 2xl: between sections on tablet
96px  (6rem)     — 3xl: between sections on desktop
128px (8rem)     — 4xl: hero padding, premium section spacing
```

### Layout Principles

#### Macro Whitespace (Section Level)

- Section padding: Minimum 96px (desktop), 64px (tablet), 48px (mobile) vertical
- Hero section: 128-160px top/bottom padding minimum
- Between major content blocks: 96-128px
- Footer: Generous top padding (96px+) to create breathing room

#### Micro Whitespace (Element Level)

- Between heading and paragraph: 16-24px
- Between paragraphs: 24px
- Card internal padding: 32-48px
- Between grid items: 24-32px

#### Content Width

- Maximum content width: 1200-1440px
- Text measure (line length): 60-75 characters maximum
- Narrow content column for readability: 680-720px
- Full-width imagery to break rhythm and create drama

#### Grid System

- 12-column grid with 24-32px gutters
- Asymmetric layouts (e.g., 5/7 or 4/8 splits) create visual interest
- Allow elements to break the grid occasionally for emphasis
- Use negative space as a compositional element, not leftover space

### Premium Layout Patterns from Award-Winning Architecture Sites

| Pattern | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Hero + Statement | Full-bleed image with single statement overlay | Selldorf Architects |
| Alternating Rhythm | Full-width image, then constrained text, repeating | Reload Architecture |
| Layered Scroll | Content layers in as user scrolls, creating depth | Blackbox Architectonics |
| Grid Gallery | Consistent grid with hover reveals | Gary Todd Architecture |
| Single-Page Narrative | Linear storytelling through continuous scroll | SITE Architecture |
| Offset Asymmetry | Elements intentionally offset from center | Studio MEMM |

## 5. Brand Voice & Messaging for Craftsman Positioning

### Brand Archetype: The Master Craftsman

Cheolmoksu's positioning sits at the intersection of artisan tradition and modern engineering. The brand voice should communicate:

- **Quiet confidence** — Let the work speak; never oversell
- **Technical authority** — Precise language about materials, processes, engineering
- **Human warmth** — Accessible expertise, not cold corporate speak
- **Transparency** — "We show everything" philosophy (aligned with YouTube presence)

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Do | Do Not |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| Confident | "We build steel houses that last generations" | "We are one of the leading providers of..." |
| Precise | "6mm steel frame, earthquake-resistant to magnitude 7" | "High-quality materials and advanced techniques" |
| Warm | "Your home deserves a builder who cares about every weld" | "Our commitment to customer satisfaction..." |
| Transparent | "Watch every step of the build on our YouTube channel" | "Trust our proven process" |
| Understated | "Thirty years of steel. Every joint matters." | "The BEST steel house builder in Korea!!!" |

### Messaging Framework

#### Tagline Options

- "철골의 정석" (The Standard in Steel) — positions as the definitive authority
- "보이는 집짓기" (Visible House Building) — leverages YouTube transparency
- "강철로 짓는 장인의 집" (A Craftsman's House, Built in Steel) — combines craft + material

#### Value Proposition Structure

1. **Primary**: Craftsman-quality steel houses with complete build transparency
2. **Supporting**: Every construction step documented and shared publicly
3. **Proof**: YouTube channel showing real builds, real timelines, real costs

#### Key Messages by Audience

| Audience | Core Message |
|----------|-------------|
| Homeowner (quality-focused) | "Your home is only as strong as the hands that build it" |
| Homeowner (transparency-seeking) | "Watch your house being built, from foundation to finish" |
| Homeowner (investment-minded) | "Steel frames last 100+ years. Your home is your legacy." |

### Content Tone Guidelines

- **Headlines**: Short, declarative, confident. 3-7 words.
- **Body copy**: Conversational but knowledgeable. Use specific numbers over vague claims.
- **Technical sections**: Precise specifications with plain-language explanations.
- **Testimonials**: Real stories, real names, real project photos.
- **YouTube integration**: Reference the channel as proof of transparency, not as marketing.

## 6. Award-Winning Architecture Website Examples

### Case Studies

#### Selldorf Architects
- **Design approach**: Restrained, elegant, and precise
- **Key feature**: Minimalist presentation signals sophistication
- **Takeaway**: Let project photography dominate; keep UI invisible

#### Blackbox Architectonics
- **Design approach**: Layered content that mirrors architectural depth
- **Key feature**: Bold hero image + modern typography, minimal style
- **Layout**: Rows layering featured work with CTAs, mixing F- and Z-patterns
- **Takeaway**: Layout structure can reinforce design philosophy

#### Reload Architecture
- **Design approach**: Minimalist with substantial whitespace
- **Key feature**: Simple typefaces emphasizing form and function
- **Takeaway**: Restraint is premium; let whitespace do the work

#### 988 Design Studio
- **Design approach**: Brand identity reinforcement through consistent motifs
- **Key feature**: Number "3" repeated in logo, navigation, and gallery grid rows
- **Takeaway**: Find one brand element and repeat it systematically

#### SITE Architecture
- **Design approach**: Single-page narrative structure
- **Key feature**: Smooth animations + crisp images in linear storytelling
- **Takeaway**: Architecture websites can be experienced like walking through a building

#### Studio MEMM
- **Design approach**: Asymmetric, offset elements
- **Key feature**: Off-center squares and overlapping elements mirror architectural work
- **Takeaway**: Digital design should reflect the physical design philosophy

### Universal Design Patterns Across Top Sites

1. **Portfolio-first**: Projects are the hero content, not company descriptions
2. **Full-bleed imagery**: Edge-to-edge photos create immersion
3. **Minimal navigation**: 3-5 top-level items maximum
4. **Scroll-driven storytelling**: Content reveals progressively on scroll
5. **Awards validation**: Dedicated awards sections for third-party credibility
6. **Restrained animation**: Subtle transitions, never flashy
7. **Responsive excellence**: Mobile experience is not an afterthought
8. **Contact accessibility**: Clear, simple path to inquiry from any page
